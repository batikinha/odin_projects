Whether for breakfast or an afternoon snack, an easy and delicious cheese bread recipe with sweet cassava starch is always welcome. Originally from Minas Gerais and beloved by Brazilians, this cheese bread uses sweet cassava starch, giving the balls an elastic dough that stretches like melted cheese! If you're making it for breakfast with a cup of coffee, you can prepare the dough the night before and store it in the refrigerator, just letting it bake before eating. This way, it will be warm, and the whole house will be happy to wake up to the smell of freshly baked cheese bread. This recipe is very easy to prepare; anyone can make it, regardless of their level of cooking experience. It yields 40 servings! See how to make it and get ready to have a new favorite snack time recipe!

  1. 500 g of sweet cassava starch
  2. 250 ml of whole milk
  3. 1 level tablespoon of salt
  4. 1 full plate (350 g) of semi-cured cheese and/or mozzarella grated on a grater (the more cheese, the tastier the bread will be)
  5. 1 or 2 eggs
  6. 1/2 cup of oil
  7. 1 package of grated parmesan cheese

1. First, heat the milk and oil in a saucepan. Turn off the heat immediately as soon as it starts to boil (you'll see bubbles rising from the milk).
2 In a large bowl, add the tapioca flour and salt and mix well. Then, pour the contents of the still-hot saucepan over the pan and mix well, first with a spoon and then with your hand.
3 Next, add the grated cheese, a little of the cheese from the dish, and 1 egg. Continue mixing well.
4 Add the remaining cheese and check that the dough has a good texture, neither too oily nor too dry.
5 If it feels too dry, add another egg. If it feels too oily, add a little more tapioca flour.
6 The dough should pull away from the bowl and also from your hand.
7 Taste the dough and see if it has enough salt; some people like to add a little more salt.
8 Now just roll into balls and place them on the baking sheet, leaving a small space between each loaf.
9 There's no need to grease the baking sheet.
10 Bake in the oven at medium temperature (230°C) until lightly golden.
